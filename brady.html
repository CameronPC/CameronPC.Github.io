Setting

A victorian-style city set on the fulcrum of the world which stretches almost down to the core. The bottom layers don't experience much light at all. The city itself is massive in scale, more akin to an entire state than a city. It has been built upon itself recursively due to limited space. Stretching the bounds of physical space, Pale Barrens stunt the outward growth of the city. The Pale Barrens themselves are a wasteland, scorched frequently by the celestic lights, while just past that lies the edge of the world, where the sky touches ground. Nobody has survived to see this far, however.

The City and the Pale Barrens

The City of Light sits directly center of the world, with the Pale Barrens at the extremes. Due to it's massive size, the climate of the City of Light can change drastically depending on location, both horizontal or vertical, however anywhere within the city is considered habitable by humans. The Pale Barrens existing outside of the city limits, while they hold many sought after resources, also are terribly caustic, capable of inflicting physical harm on anyone stupid enough to venture outwards. Under direct sun/moonlight, an individual will begin to go through a process akin to 'melting'. After approximately seven seconds of direct exposure, the individual will be effectively dead, and will turn into a Dreg in tandum with the current celestial cycle.

Celestial Bodies and Cycles

The City of Light experiences day and night differently. There are five varying suns and moons, which cycle through on a daily basis. Weeks are five days long due to this fact. Each celestial body has a unique appearance as well as special properties, both represented in their light (see: Magic), and an associated mineral. The celestial bodies are as follows:

A multitude of religions center around the celestial bodies, with the most prevelant being the Magistry due to their close ties with city Nobility and their fiercesome Lightbearers. None of these religions are explicitly true, though religions without belief in any of the celestial bodies are usually considered taboo. The reason for such ferverent belief is the ability for some individuals to wield light for themselves. This process is known as Lightbearing (see: Magic). A common belief is that each pair (a sun and a moon) are equal and opposites. This is also expressed in Lightbearing.

Politics

The main political system of that in the City of Light follows a monarch, known by the title of 'Autarch', and a series of noble houses. The nobles houses are not determined by blood, but rather by financial standing; they provide vital services to the city, such as lumber or stone. A heavy system of political intrigue has set in an attempt to secure more power, with nobles often hosting lavish parties or dueling enemies. Generally, however, the assassination of a noble will be met with persecution by the Autarch and the Praesidium, the city peacekeepers. This has only compelled the nobles to plot against each other in secret The Magistry is often used for mercenary work for the houses, and by the Autarch. Otherwise they are a neutral third party, content with their power in the city.

Lightbearing

Summary

Lightbearing is a genetic ability in which an individual is able to absorb light from the various celestial bodies and use it to gain supernatural abilities Each celestial body grants a different ability; a notable trend is that a sun and moon will provide abilities that are linked in some way. The amount of light a user has stored is measured in what is known as a 'Lum'. Conversely, the drain a given ability has on volume of light stored is what's called 'Dispersion Rate'. Any given Lightbearer can store around 100 Lums before experiecing adverse effects, such as melting.

Furthermore, certain minerals can also store a charge of light, providing a means to store different types of light. The disperation rate, and volume of light a mineral is able to store is dependant on size and mineral type. A notable connection has been made between these minerals and a linking celestial body; there are ten in total.

It should be noted that Lightbearers have been unable to draw upon the power of Mysus or Axien.

The Interior Body

The Interior Body (also know as: The Interior) is used to define those within the main assembly of politics in the City of Light. Simple paper workers and bureaucrats that keep the system flowing from a legal standpoint. They are also referred to as: "The Autarch's Left Hand".

Scribes

The lowest rank of the Interior Body. They are messengers responsible for relaying information across the city. They are also used as intermediaries between the Interior Body, the Magistry, and Noble Houses.

Interpreters

A broad position, used to represent most of the general paperworkers. These are the people who apply the laws of the city across the numerous districts, interpreting the law into a form best suited to run parts of the city

Overseers

Overbosses of the Interpreters. There is one Overseer per district, which report to a Prime Overseer. There are twenty Prime Overseers in the city at any given time. Prime Overseers report directly to the Autarch.

Autarch

The Autarch is the supreme ruler of the City of Light, and head of the Interior Body. They have complete power over the laws of the city. Their word is final.

The Praetariat

The Praetariat is the disciplinary body in the city. They are peacekeepers, and a warning against those that would seek to deface the Autarch or the city. In many ways, they are the balance that keep all the other factions in check. While there are Lightbearers in the ranks, the Pratariat focuses more on physical skill in combat. They are also referred to as: "The Autarch's Right Hand".

Guards

Lowest Rank of the Pratariat. Armed with a spear, they serve as the first line of defense in many situations. Despite their low rank, they are fully trained and are able to best most non-Lightbearer combatants.

Captains

Captains are the next step above a traditional Guard, showing exemplary combat and/or leadership skills. A traditional unit of the Praetariat consists of five individuals: four Guards, and a Captain. Instead of a spear, Captains are armed with a pair of swords: a standard shortsword, and an estoc. A flintlock pistol is also used to bolster their arsenal. They are trained in both armed and unarmed combat.

Commanders

The third highest rank in the Praetariat. There is one Commander per district, and are brought to the front lines only in case of emergency. They are very deft in combat, able to go toe to toe with some Lightbearers.

Praetor

Members of the Autarch's inner-circle. There are only three Praetors, responsible for overseeing the entire military. Any given Praetor has showcased mastery of both combat and strategy, and as such are some of the most difficult individuals to kill because of this. Currently, there are two regular Praetors, and one Arch-Praetor (see: Special Ranks)

Special Ranks

A designation given to Lightbearers in the Praetariat. This does not override any current rank. For example, Lightbearing members would be referred to as: Lancer Guard, Lancer Captain, Special Commander, and Arch Praetor respectively. In terms of rank, a Lancer Guard technically outranks a regular Guard, but is still beneath a regular Captain.

Autarch

Head of the Praetariat. While direct interference is rare, the Autarch outranks even the Arch Praetor, and as such their orders are final.

The Magistry

The Magistry is not under direct control of the Autarch, and therefore a third-party institution. While technically a religious institution, the Magistry provides many services to the city, placing them firmly in a position of some power. Their ability to produce powerful Lightbearers is a useful tool to many people, including both the Noble Houses and the Autarch. Most members of the Magistry are nonbinary, believing this makes them more 'divine' and brings them closer to godliness.

Acolytes

Simply a member of the church. Either an active or passive participant in the Magistry. They provide no service other than their faith.

Curator

Lightbearing member of the church. Provides services dealing with Lightbearing. Typically used to bolster workforces for the Noble Houses.

Inquisitor

Lightbearing member of the church. They provide mercenary services for those who provide the right price. Often used to supplement the Praetoriat, although they also have been used in several assassination plots regarding the nobility. Both the Noble Houses and the Magistry deny these claims.

The Noble Houses

The Noble Houses provide various important economic services to the city. While these positions are non-political, the resources they provide allow the City of Light to run, and as such, it's in the Autarch's best interest to keep them satisifed. The Noble Houses are very cutthroat, with members desperate to secure as much power as they can, either by employing workers in the depths of the city, or trying to steal the positions of other houses. Many assassinations have been enacted because of this, only few of which could be reasonably proven.

Due to the instrinsic nature of the Houses, each one follows a different structure, depending on the current leadership, and house size.
